Materials

Quality repairs start with quality materials. Below are the tools and supplies we use in our own workshop and recommend to anyone tackling a DIY bat repair. Each item has been tested on dozens of bats over multiple seasons. Click any item to ask us about availability or request a recommendation for your specific repair.

Repair Supplies

Bat Repair Glue

A strong wood glue specifically formulated for English willow. It dries clear, bonds quickly under clamping pressure, and forms a flexible joint that can absorb the impact of a fast delivery without cracking again. We never use general-purpose super glue on a bat as it dries too brittle.

Ask About This Item

Fibre Tape

A thin, transparent reinforcement tape applied to the face of the bat after a crack repair. It distributes impact across a wider area and stops cracks from spreading along the grain. Many professional players use fibre tape on the face of a brand new bat as a preventive measure.

Ask About This Item

Cane Handles

Premium cane handles for handle replacement jobs, available in Singapore and Sarawak cane in multiple grades. The cane is bonded with rubber strips for shock absorption. Choosing the right grade for your style of play makes a huge difference in pickup, balance, and feel at the crease.

Ask About This Item

Toe Guard

A precut rubber toe guard that protects the bottom of the bat from moisture and ground impact. Glued onto a clean, sanded toe in under 30 minutes, it can dramatically extend the life of any bat. We strongly recommend fitting one to every new bat before its first match.

Ask About This Item

Raw Linseed Oil

Raw linseed oil for conditioning natural unsealed willow. Apply a thin coat with a soft cloth before knocking-in and again every few months during the season. It keeps the wood from drying out and helps prevent surface cracking. Boiled linseed oil is too thick and not recommended for bats.

Ask About This Item

Bat Mallet

A wooden mallet shaped and weighted specifically for knocking in new bats. The flat striking face compresses the willow fibres evenly across the face and edges of the blade. Using a regular hammer will dent the wood and cause damage, so always use a proper bat mallet.
